Krishna is a major deity in Hinduism, revered as the eighth avatar of Vishnu and also worshiped as the Supreme God in his own right. He is known for his compassion, protection, and love, and is a central figure in the Bhagavad Gita and the Mahabharata. Krishna's name and various epithets, like Mohan (the enchanter) and Govinda (the chief herdsman), reflect his diverse roles and attributes. Here's a more detailed look at Krishna:

  • Divine Figure:
    Krishna is considered a divine figure, an incarnation of Vishnu, and also a Supreme God in some traditions.
  • Central Figure: He is a key character in the Mahabharata, the Bhagavata Purana, and the Bhagavad Gita, where he is portrayed as a warrior, hero, teacher, and philosopher.
  • Various Representations: Krishna is depicted in various ways, from a playful child-god to a divine lover, a prankster, and a universal supreme being.
  • Worship and Devotion: Krishna's worship is widespread, particularly in the Vaishnavism tradition of Hinduism, and his birthday, Janmashtami, is celebrated with great fervor.
  • Teachings and Philosophy: Krishna's teachings, particularly in the Bhagavad Gita, offer profound wisdom on dharma, karma, and the nature of reality.
  • Symbolism and Significance: Krishna's attributes, like his dark complexion (meaning "all attractive" or "dark blue"), and his association with the flute and the gopis, hold significant symbolic meaning.
